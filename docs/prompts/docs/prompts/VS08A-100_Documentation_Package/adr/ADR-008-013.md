# ADR-008-013 — Tenant Lifecycle

**Status:** Accepted

**Engine:** VS08 – License, Subscription & Tenant Management Engine

**Milestone:** VS08A – Tenant Foundation

---

# Context

A Tenant is the primary commercial and security boundary of the CAP Platform.

Every subscription, workspace, license, feature entitlement, and installed application belongs to a Tenant.

A controlled lifecycle is required to ensure predictable onboarding, operational management, suspension, and retirement while preserving historical data and auditability.

---

# Decision

The Tenant lifecycle shall consist of four states:

```text
Provisioning
        │
        ▼
Active
        │
        ├──────────────┐
        ▼              │
Suspended ─────────────┘
        │
        ▼
Archived
```

---

# Lifecycle Rules

## Provisioning

A newly created Tenant enters the **Provisioning** state.

Characteristics:

* Tenant exists.
* Metadata may be edited.
* No Workspaces.
* No Licenses.
* No Subscriptions.
* Runtime access prohibited.

---

## Active

The Tenant is operational.

Capabilities:

* Workspaces may be created.
* Applications may be installed.
* Memberships may be assigned.
* Licenses may be activated.
* Runtime execution permitted.

---

## Suspended

The Tenant remains in the platform but operational access is disabled.

Characteristics:

* Runtime execution blocked.
* Logins blocked.
* Data preserved.
* Administrative metadata may still be updated.

Suspension is reversible.

---

## Archived

Archived is the terminal lifecycle state.

Characteristics:

* No runtime access.
* No login.
* No modifications.
* Historical data retained.
* No reactivation.

---

# Valid Transitions

| From         | To        | Allowed |
| ------------ | --------- | ------- |
| Provisioning | Active    | ✅       |
| Active       | Suspended | ✅       |
| Suspended    | Active    | ✅       |
| Suspended    | Archived  | ✅       |

All other transitions are prohibited.

---

# Consequences

This lifecycle provides:

* Safe onboarding
* Controlled suspension
* Predictable retirement
* Complete auditability
* Preservation of historical data

---

# Alternatives Considered

### Immediate Active

Rejected.

Provisioning allows validation and setup before production use.

### Hard Delete

Rejected.

Violates CAP audit and retention principles.

---

# Decision Summary

Tenant lifecycle is defined as:

**Provisioning → Active → Suspended → Archived**

with explicit transition rules and irreversible archival.
